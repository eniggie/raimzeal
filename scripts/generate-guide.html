What is RAIMZEAL?

RAIMZEAL is a premium fitness platform built as a Progressive Web App (PWA) and native mobile companion. It combines workout tracking, nutrition logging, AI coaching, community engagement, and health analytics into a single seamless experience.

The platform was created by Dr. Ephraim Oviawe — fitness advocate, medical professional, author, and musician — with the goal of making elite-level health guidance accessible to everyone.

✍️ About the Creator

Dr. Ephraim Oviawe is a multidisciplinary professional whose passion spans medicine, fitness, music, and writing. RAIMZEAL reflects his belief that holistic health — physical, mental, and nutritional — is the foundation of a fulfilling life. Learn more about his work including books and music at linktr.ee/Raimzy.

Platform Overview

📱 Mobile App

Full-featured Expo React Native app with barcode scanner, pedometer step tracking, camera integration, and native notifications. Works on iOS, Android, and web.

🌐 Web App (PWA)

Installable on any device via browser. Full parity with the mobile experience. Available at www.raimzeal.com. Add to home screen for an app-like experience.

⚡ Technology Stack
React Native + Expo TypeScript Ovia AI (Streaming) Supabase Auth Open Food Facts API Node.js API Server PostgreSQL Vite PWA

🥗 Section 5

Nutrition Tracking

Log every meal, track macros in real time, scan barcodes, and discover the best foods for your goals.

Daily Overview

🔥 Calorie Ring

Large ring showing today's calorie intake vs. your goal. Protein, Carbs, and Fat progress bars update live as you log meals.

📊 Macro Breakdown

Protein (g), Carbs (g), and Fat (g) shown as coloured progress bars with current/goal labels — green, yellow, and purple respectively.

Logging a Meal

  1. 1
    Tap the Search Bar
    Search "Search food by name…" — results appear in real time from the Open Food Facts database (millions of foods).
  2. 2
    Select a Food
    Tap any result to open the Add Food modal — it shows calories, protein, carbs, fat, and a serving size input.
  3. 3
    Set Amount
    For API foods, type the exact grams or ml. Nutrition chips update live as you type. For packaged foods with a serving size, adjust servings with +/− buttons.
  4. 4
    Choose Meal Category
    Select Breakfast, Lunch, Dinner, or Snack from the dropdown.
  5. 5
    Tap "Add Food"
    The meal is logged instantly. Macros and the calorie ring update. The food is added to your Recent Foods list.

Barcode Scanner

Tap the green Scan button in the top-right corner to open the barcode scanner. Point your camera at any food product's barcode — the app fetches full nutritional data from Open Food Facts instantly and opens the Add Food modal pre-filled.

Camera permission is required for the barcode scanner. The app will prompt you the first time you tap Scan. This permission is only used for scanning — no photos are stored.

Smart Filter Chips

Filter search results by nutritional profile with one tap:

Filter Default Threshold What it shows
⚡ High Protein ≥ 15g protein per 100g Foods with significant protein content
🔥 Low Calorie ≤ 150 kcal per 100g Lower energy-density foods
💧 Low Fat ≤ 5g fat per 100g Leaner food options
🌾 Low Carb ≤ 10g carbs per 100g Reduced carbohydrate foods
⚙️ Custom Thresholds

Each filter chip has a small settings icon. Tap it to open the threshold editor — adjust the gram or calorie value using ± step buttons or type it directly. A Reset button reverts to the default. Thresholds are saved and persist between sessions. Chip labels update live, e.g. "High Protein ≥25g".

Result count badges: Inactive filter chips show a small badge with the number of results that would remain if you activated that filter — helping you choose the most useful filter before tapping.

Favourites & Recent Foods

⭐ Favourites

Tap the star icon next to any food to save it as a favourite. Favourites appear in their own tab for instant re-logging without searching.

🕐 Recent Foods

Your 10 most recently logged foods appear below the meal log for quick re-use. Tap any to open the Add Food modal pre-filled.

History Tab

Switch to the History tab to view past days' meals organised by date. Tap any day to expand and see all foods logged. You can re-log any historical entry or delete it.

Editing & Deleting Meals

In today's meal log, each entry has an edit (✏️) button. Tap it to adjust the amount or swap the meal category. Long-press or tap the delete icon to remove a meal entry — a confirmation prompt appears before deletion.

Progress

Visualise your transformation — weight trends, calorie history, streaks, and unlockable achievements.

Stats Row

🔥 Streak

Consecutive days with at least one workout logged. Keep it alive — Ovia AI celebrates milestones at 7, 30, and 90 days.

💪 Workouts

Total sessions completed this week, alongside a cumulative all-time count.

📅 This Week

Number of active days in the current calendar week.

An interactive line chart of your body weight over time. Switch between 1M, 3M, and 6M time ranges. Hover (web) or tap data points (mobile) to see the exact weight and date. The trend line shows your overall direction.

Weekly Calories Bar Chart

A bar chart showing calorie intake for each day of the past 7 days. Bars are colour-coded green for days under goal and a lighter shade for days over goal. Use this to identify patterns and spot problem days.

  • ★ Macro Master — Hit all three macro targets in a single day
  • ★ Social Star — Post in the community 5 times
  • ★ Century — Complete 100 total workouts
➕ Add Measurement

Tap the green + Measure button to log a new body measurement entry directly from the Progress screen. This updates all charts immediately.

Monitor your daily movement, weekly activity, and progress toward WHO fitness benchmarks.

Today's Activity Panel

📏 Section 10

Body Measurements

Track your physical transformation with precision — beyond just the scale.

Stats at a Glance

⚖️ Current Weight

Your most recently logged body weight, shown prominently at the top.

📊 BMI

Automatically calculated from your height and latest weight. Displayed with a category label: Underweight, Healthy, Overweight, or Obese.

↘ Last Change

Difference between your two most recent entries — shown as kg lost or gained with a colour indicator (green = loss, red = gain).

📋 Entries

Total number of measurement logs ever recorded.

Logging a New Entry

Tap + Add Entry in the top right. A modal appears with fields for:

  • ✓ Weight (kg) — required field
  • ✓ Chest (cm) — optional
  • ✓ Waist (cm) — optional
  • ✓ Hips (cm) — optional
  • ✓ Arms (cm) — optional
  • ✓ Thighs (cm) — optional

History List

Every past entry is listed chronologically with date, weight, and a delta badge (e.g. ↘ -0.3). Tap any entry to expand it and view the full body measurements logged that day. Entries can be deleted individually.

Best practice: Log your weight at the same time each day — ideally in the morning after waking, before eating or drinking — for the most accurate trend data.
